# Scenario Pack: Microsoft 365 Copilot

> **Version:** v0.1  
> **Technology:** Microsoft 365 Copilot  
> **Scope:** Role-based adoption guidance across core organizational functions  

---

## Overview

This Scenario Pack provides role-based AI adoption guidance for Microsoft 365 Copilot deployments. It maps Copilot capabilities to real-world work activities across five core organizational roles, providing the use-case content that makes Copilot adoption feel relevant to individual employees — not just to the organization as a whole.

This pack is the primary content source consumed by the Role-Based Scenarios skill and the Adoption Agent when generating pilot activation content.

---

## How to Use This Scenario Pack

Each role section below documents:

- **Role Description** — Who this person is and what they do
- **Primary Work Challenges** — What slows them down or creates friction
- **Top Copilot Use Cases** — The most impactful ways Copilot helps this role
- **Sample Prompts** — Illustrative examples of what this person might ask Copilot
- **Adoption Considerations** — What to watch for during enablement of this role

## Role: Executives

### Role Description

Senior leaders (C-suite, VPs, Directors) responsible for organizational strategy, decision-making, stakeholder communication, and team leadership. Typically time-constrained and managing high information volume.

### Primary Work Challenges

- Overwhelming volume of documents, emails, and meeting content to review
- Need to communicate consistently and effectively across multiple audiences
- Time spent drafting presentations, briefings, and strategic documents
- Staying informed across many concurrent initiatives

### Top Copilot Use Cases

| Use Case | Copilot Capability | Business Value |
|---|---|---|
| Meeting briefing preparation | Summarize recent emails, Teams conversations, and documents about an upcoming meeting | Save 30–60 minutes of preparation time per major meeting |
| Communication drafting | Draft executive updates, board memos, and company-wide announcements | Faster, more consistent leadership communications |
| Document summarization | Summarize lengthy reports, proposals, and strategy documents | Rapid comprehension without reading full documents |
| Town hall preparation | Draft key messages and talking points from strategic documents | Better-prepared, more impactful employee communications |
| Decision support | Ask Copilot to summarize pros/cons from relevant documents before a decision | Better-informed decisions with less manual research |

### Sample Prompts

- *"Summarize the key points from last week's sales review and highlight any items requiring my decision."*
- *"Draft a message to all employees announcing our Q3 results and the three priorities for Q4."*
- *"What were the main concerns raised in the last three board meetings?"*
- *"Create a one-page executive summary of the attached strategy document."*

### Adoption Considerations

- Executives often have assistants — include assistants in training as they may use Copilot on behalf of executives
- Focus on high-impact, time-saving use cases; avoid feature overload
- Executive champions have outsized influence — visible usage by leaders accelerates adoption across the organization

## Role: Sales

### Role Description

Account executives, sales managers, and business development representatives responsible for pipeline management, client relationships, proposals, and revenue generation. Highly email and meeting intensive.

### Primary Work Challenges

- High volume of client communications requiring fast, personalized responses
- Time spent writing proposals, summaries, and follow-up emails after meetings
- Keeping CRM and records up to date after customer interactions
- Preparing for client meetings and researching accounts

### Top Copilot Use Cases

| Use Case | Copilot Capability | Business Value |
|---|---|---|
| Meeting follow-up drafting | Generate a follow-up email from meeting notes and action items | Save 20–30 minutes after each client meeting |
| Proposal drafting | Draft proposal sections based on client requirements documents | Faster first drafts; more time for value-add customization |
| Account briefing | Summarize recent communications and documents before a client call | Better-prepared sales conversations |
| Email response drafting | Draft responses to client enquiries from context | Faster response times; improved client experience |
| Sales pipeline summary | Summarize the state of open opportunities from recent activity | Faster pipeline reviews with less admin work |

### Sample Prompts

- *"Draft a follow-up email to [Client] summarizing the key points from today's call and the next steps we agreed."*
- *"Summarize all communications with [Client] from the past 30 days and highlight any open items."*
- *"Draft the executive summary section of a proposal for a company in the retail sector needing [solution]."*
- *"Create a meeting agenda for tomorrow's quarterly review with [Client]."*

### Adoption Considerations

- Sales teams often have the highest ROI from Copilot due to the volume of communications they generate
- Focus initial training on meeting follow-up and email drafting — highest immediate time savings
- Reinforce that Copilot outputs should be reviewed and personalized before sending to clients

## Role: HR

### Role Description

HR business partners, talent acquisition specialists, and people operations professionals responsible for recruitment, employee experience, policy management, and organizational development.

### Primary Work Challenges

- Time-consuming job description drafting and updating
- High volume of employee queries about policies, benefits, and processes
- Creating and maintaining training and onboarding materials
- Synthesizing feedback from surveys and interviews

### Top Copilot Use Cases

| Use Case | Copilot Capability | Business Value |
|---|---|---|
| Job description drafting | Draft job descriptions from role requirements | Faster, more consistent job postings |
| Policy Q&A | Answer employee questions about HR policies from documents | Faster employee self-service; reduced HR query volume |
| Onboarding material creation | Draft onboarding guides, welcome communications, and checklists | Better new hire experience; consistent onboarding |
| Interview guide creation | Generate structured interview questions from job descriptions | More consistent, fair interview processes |
| Survey synthesis | Summarize employee survey feedback and identify themes | Faster insights from employee listening data |

### Sample Prompts

- *"Draft a job description for a Senior Data Analyst with experience in Power BI and Azure, based on the attached role brief."*
- *"Summarize our parental leave policy in plain language suitable for a new employee FAQ."*
- *"Create a 30-60-90 day onboarding plan for a new Sales Account Executive."*
- *"What are the top five themes from the employee engagement survey responses I've pasted below?"*

### Adoption Considerations

- HR teams often deal with sensitive employee data — reinforce data handling guidelines before activation
- Policy Q&A use cases benefit from well-organized SharePoint policy libraries; address data hygiene early
- HR professionals tend to be cautious about AI — provide extra context on what Copilot does and does not store

## Role: Finance

### Role Description

Finance analysts, controllers, FP&A professionals, and accountants responsible for financial reporting, budgeting, forecasting, and analysis. Data-intensive and deadline-driven.

### Primary Work Challenges

- Time spent formatting and narrating financial reports
- Creating presentations summarizing financial data for non-finance audiences
- Answering questions about financial policies and procedures
- Summarizing large volumes of financial documents (contracts, reports, filings)

### Top Copilot Use Cases

| Use Case | Copilot Capability | Business Value |
|---|---|---|
| Financial report narration | Convert data tables into written executive summaries | Faster reporting; clearer communication of financial results |
| Presentation drafting | Create presentation outlines and slide content from financial data | Faster board and management reporting |
| Document summarization | Summarize contracts, audit reports, and regulatory filings | Faster review of complex financial documents |
| Email drafting | Draft responses to financial enquiries and stakeholder questions | Faster stakeholder communication |
| Meeting preparation | Summarize recent financial communications before budget reviews | Better-prepared finance meetings |

### Sample Prompts

- *"Write an executive summary of our Q3 financial performance based on the attached results data."*
- *"Summarize the key provisions of the attached vendor contract, focusing on payment terms and renewal conditions."*
- *"Draft talking points for presenting the Q3 variance analysis to the management team."*
- *"What are the key differences between the two budget scenarios in the attached documents?"*

### Adoption Considerations

- Finance teams have strict data security requirements — ensure sensitivity labels and DLP policies are configured before activation
- Use cases involving financial data should emphasize that Copilot assists with communication and summarization, not calculation or audit
- Finance professionals value accuracy; set clear expectations about reviewing all Copilot outputs

## Role: Operations

### Role Description

Operations managers, project coordinators, and process owners responsible for workflow management, cross-team coordination, process documentation, and operational reporting.

### Primary Work Challenges

- High volume of status updates and coordination communications
- Creating and maintaining process documentation
- Synthesizing information from multiple teams and systems
- Meeting-heavy schedules with extensive follow-up requirements

### Top Copilot Use Cases

| Use Case | Copilot Capability | Business Value |
|---|---|---|
| Status report drafting | Draft status reports from Teams messages and meeting notes | Faster reporting; less time on admin |
| Process documentation | Create process guides and standard operating procedures | Faster documentation of operational processes |
| Meeting summarization | Summarize large multi-team meetings with action items | Faster, more consistent meeting follow-up |
| Cross-team communications | Draft project updates and announcements to multiple stakeholders | More consistent, professional cross-team communications |
| Action item tracking | Extract and organize action items from meeting transcripts | Less missed follow-up; better accountability |

### Sample Prompts

- *"Summarize this week's project status across the three work streams in the attached meeting notes."*
- *"Create a standard operating procedure for the monthly vendor invoice approval process based on our current email workflows."*
- *"Extract all action items from the attached meeting transcript and organize them by owner."*
- *"Draft a project update email to all stakeholders summarizing progress this week and flagging two key risks."*

### Adoption Considerations

- Operations teams benefit from Copilot in Teams more than any other surface — ensure Teams is fully deployed and in use before activation
- Meeting summarization is often the highest-impact first use case for operations roles
- Process documentation use cases may require access to existing process documentation in SharePoint — address content hygiene early

## Scenario Pack Notes

### Deployment Priority Guidance

When selecting the pilot cohort for a Microsoft 365 Copilot deployment, prioritize departments based on:

1. **Meeting intensity** — Roles with 6+ meetings per week gain most from meeting summarization
2. **Writing intensity** — Roles producing high volumes of written communication gain most from drafting assistance
3. **Information synthesis** — Roles that regularly summarize complex documents gain most from summarization
4. **Executive visibility** — Including executives in the pilot creates visible adoption signals that accelerate organization-wide uptake

### Use Case Selection for SMB Organizations

For smaller organizations (under 250 employees), the following use cases typically have the fastest time-to-value:

1. Meeting summarization and follow-up drafting (universal)
2. Email drafting and response (universal)
3. Document summarization (universal)
4. Role-specific scenarios from the departments in scope
