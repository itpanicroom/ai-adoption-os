# Skill: Stakeholder Mapping

> **Status:** Placeholder — not implemented in v0.1

## Purpose

The Stakeholder Mapping skill identifies, categorizes, and prioritizes all individuals and groups with a stake in the AI adoption initiative. It ensures that the right people are engaged at the right time with the right level of involvement.

## CAF Phase

**Strategy · Plan**

## Invoked By

- Strategy Agent
- Readiness Agent

## Inputs

- Organizational input profile (departments, company size, executive sponsor status)
- Scenario Pack (role-based context for which departments are most impacted)

## Expected Outputs

- Stakeholder register: list of key individuals and groups
- Influence/interest mapping for each stakeholder
- Engagement strategy for each stakeholder category
- Communication requirements per stakeholder group
- Stakeholder RACI for key adoption activities

## Key Activities

1. Identify all departments in scope for the adoption initiative
2. Map key individuals within each department (sponsors, decision-makers, end users, IT contacts)
3. Assess each stakeholder's level of influence and interest in the initiative
4. Define the engagement approach for each stakeholder category
5. Identify potential champions candidates from the stakeholder map
6. Define communication requirements for each stakeholder group

## Stakeholder Categories

| Category | Description | Engagement Level |
|---|---|---|
| Executive Sponsors | Senior leaders funding and championing the initiative | High — regular updates and decisions |
| Department Heads | Leaders of departments in scope | Medium — phase-gate approvals |
| IT / Technical Owners | Teams responsible for deployment and configuration | High — technical coordination |
| Champions | Early adopters and peer advocates | High — activation and support |
| End Users | Employees who will use the AI technology | Medium — training and communications |
| Compliance / Legal | Teams responsible for governance and risk | Medium — policy review and sign-off |

## Notes

The stakeholder map should be treated as a living document. New stakeholders will emerge as adoption progresses.
